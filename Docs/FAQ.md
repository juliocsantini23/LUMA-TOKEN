# LumaChain – Technical FAQ

Frequently asked technical questions about the **LUMA** token and this repository.

---

### 1. On which network was the LUMA token launched?

LUMA was launched on **Polygon PoS (Mainnet)**, an EVM-compatible scaling solution.

---

### 2. What is the token contract address?

- **LUMA (ERC-20) contract:** `0x453453096e669a8a48772287210098faA55bd64b`

Always verify the official address in this repository or through our official channels before interacting with any contract.

---

### 3. What is the total supply and how many decimals does the token have?

- **Total supply:** 300,000,000 LUMA  
- **Decimals:** 18

---

### 4. Is part of the supply locked?

Yes. A significant portion of the tokens is locked in **timelock** contracts, including:

- 60M LUMA – Team & Development wallet  
- 30M LUMA – Treasury & Ecosystem wallet  

The wallet and timelock contract addresses are listed in `CONTRACT_ADDRESSES.md` and in the transparency documents.

---

### 5. Where can I check supply and lock transparency?

- `Dados/Supply.json`  
- `Documentos/Transparency.md`  
- `CONTRACT_ADDRESSES.md` with the relevant addresses  

---

### 6. Does this token offer guaranteed yields or promised returns?

**No.**  
LUMA is a utility token within the planned LumaChain ecosystem.  
There is no guarantee of profitability, fixed returns, automatic profit distribution or any promise of future gains.

---

Users and interested parties should consult their own legal and financial advisors, especially in jurisdictions with specific regulation.

### 11. Who controls the team and treasury wallets?

The Team & Development and Treasury wallets are subject to **timelock** contracts, which limit movements until predefined dates.  
Any relevant change will be documented in this repository and in `Transparency.md`.
